Best GeoGuessr Alternatives

Geography games built around Street View and interactive maps have grown massively in popularity. If you're searching for the best GeoGuessr alternatives, here’s a breakdown of the top options — including competitive multiplayer experiences.

1. GeoGuessr

GeoGuessr is the most well-known Street View geography game. Players are dropped into a random real-world location and must guess where they are on a map. Points are awarded based on distance accuracy.

It focuses heavily on solo exploration, map knowledge, and deduction skills.

2. GeoBrawl

GeoBrawl introduces a strategic multiplayer twist. Instead of random locations, one player becomes the Picker and deliberately selects a Street View location for others to guess.

This adds a chess like aspect to the game, and competitive depth. Roles rotate each round, making it a fast paced multiplayer geography game designed for private rooms and live competition which utilizes both geography guessing skills and competitive logic in the game.

3. City Guesser

City Guesser replaces Google Street View with real world video footage. Players watch walking or driving clips and try to identify the city or country.

It emphasizes cultural awareness and visual clues rather than map navigation.

4. Seterra

Seterra is a traditional geography quiz game. Instead of Street View exploration, it focuses on labeling countries, capitals, and regions on a static map.

It's ideal for memorization and educational practice.

Which Geography Game Is Best?

If you enjoy solo exploration and traditional Street View guessing, GeoGuessr remains the classic option.

If you want a competitive multiplayer geography game with strategic depth, GeoBrawl offers a unique twist that transforms guessing into a tactical battle.

For educational quizzes or video-based guessing, alternatives like Seterra or City Guesser may be a better fit.
